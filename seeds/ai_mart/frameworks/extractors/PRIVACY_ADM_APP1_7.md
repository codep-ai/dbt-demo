## Why this framework

Schedule 1 of the **Privacy and Other Legislation Amendment Act 2024 (Cth)**
inserts new transparency requirements into APP 1, commencing **10 December
2026**. Where an APP entity uses a computer program to make, or substantially
assist in making, a decision that could reasonably be expected to
**significantly affect the rights or interests of an individual**, its APP
Privacy Policy must disclose:

- the kinds of decisions made,
- the kinds of personal information used,
- the existence of automated decision making.

The OAIC has compliance and infringement-notice powers (commenced 11 Dec
2024). This is the **single hardest deadline in AU AI governance** — a
universal trigger across every APP entity using AI in advice, credit, claims,
hiring, fraud or eligibility decisioning.

**This is the explicit Dec 2026 commercial wedge.** Carved out as its own
`framework_code` (separate from `AU_PRIVACY_APPS`) so customers can filter
to "Dec 2026 readiness controls only" for the 90-day onboarding pitch.

## Notes

- Effective date set to 2026-12-10 (commencement of Schedule 1 ADM
  amendments).
- Amendments apply prospectively to decisions made on or after 10 Dec 2026,
  irrespective of when the underlying algorithm or data collection arose.
- Small business APP exemption (turnover < $3M) still applies — many small
  AFSLs are exempt from the Privacy Act entirely; check entity status before
  asserting applicability.
- The next tranche of Privacy reforms is expected to add a substantive right
  for individuals to obtain meaningful information about ADM decisions — re-
  author this framework when that lands.
